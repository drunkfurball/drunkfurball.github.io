About me

Howdy! You've stumbled upon my personal portfolio page. Sadly, we weren't expecting visitors so soon. You can come back later, and hopefully when you do, I'll have something really impressive to show you. Need some suggestions on where to go? Try my very addicting, super simple video poker game.

You're still here? Well, this is awkward. How about I tell you a bit about myself? Yeah?

I'm a single dad, currently working as a telemarketer--FREE PIZZA!!! Sorry, thought I was losing you. Yes, I'm one of those annoying folks that calls you during dinner and trys to convince you that you need to change your electric supplier or whatever. But I don't enjoy it. So I'm taking the advice of my computer science teacher, and pursuing a career in code.

I began my interest in programming in the seventh grade, when I took the only coding course my high school, a public school in rural Minnesota with a graduating class of 80, had to offer. Honestly, I'm just glad they offered something.

I guess I showed a real knack for this stuff, because I completed, and aced that coding course in a few weeks (the course was designed to be a semester long).

I looked into the opportunities that computer science offered, but at the time of my graduation, I decided I wanted to do something different. I found it a little unsettling that my guidance counceller was pitching CS career paths to everyone in my class, all 80 of them, and at the time, the internet still screamed like a cat in a lawn mower when you logged on.
