About me

Howdy! You've stumbled upon my personal portfolio page. Sadly, we weren't expecting visitors so soon. You can come back later, and hopefully when you do, I'll have something really impressive to show you. Need some suggestions on where to go? Try my very addicting, super simple video poker game.

You're still here? Well, this is awkward. How about I tell you a bit about myself? Yeah?

I'm a single dad, currently working as a telemarketer--FREE PIZZA!!! Sorry, thought I was losing you. Yes, I'm one of those annoying folks that calls you during dinner and tries to convince you that you need to change your electric supplier or whatever. But I don't enjoy it. So I'm taking the advice my computer science teacher gave me almost two decades ago, and pursuing a career in code.

I've had an interest in computers since my parents bought our first home computer when I was eight. It had Windows 3.1, one of those floppy drives with the wide flat diskettes, and not much else. I learned DOS commands on it.

I'm well versed in HTML, CSS and JavaScript, which has been a hobby of mine since the 6th grade. I also know Python, and have spent a bit of time in Java and the C languages. I worked as a web content manager for my small town newspaper and webmaster for my highschool before going off to college (to pursue psychology, trucking, nursing, and spent a short while as a photographer, and a lot of time in retail).

Through the years, I've done well to keep my skills current. I'm a participant at freeCodeCamp.org, I also moderate for their Facebook groups freeCodeCamp Earth and freeCodeCamp = 'You Can Do This!';, and I'm the local leader of the Facebook group freeCodeCamp(Duluth, MN);.
